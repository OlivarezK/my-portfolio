Projects Certificates Final Report

Overview of Practicum Engagement

The learning path is the track that the student took for this practicum engagement. In this track, the student took online courses from LinkedIn Learning to acquire knowledge that should have been gained in training under a host company. Similar to the HTE track, there is also a required number of hours that should be rendered which is three hundred and twenty-four (324) hours. But in the learning path track the engagement hours are doubled which means learners are only required to have at least one hundred and sixty-two (162) hours of engagement with their chosen courses. By the end of the learning path engagement, the student was able to have a total of one hundred and seventy-one (171) hours which totals to three hundred and forty-two (342) rendered hours.

The learning path that the student proposed consists of required and specialization learning modules. These modules contain a variety of courses that covers learning about both soft skills and hard skills to prepare the student for the work environment.

The design thinking module have courses that discuss topics on creative thinking, critical thinking, and innovation skills. These courses does not only talk about the importance of these skills but also ways and exercises on how to improve them. The project management module discusses the waterfall and agile project management methodologies. The courses under this module provides the necessary information that learners need to be familiar with such as the use cases, processes, and advantages of the two methodologies. The third module, workplace management, has courses about different business etiquettes. This includes proper communication both in person and other means such as emails, time management practices, and the 5S methodology.

The specialization learning module on the other hand has courses that leans toward learning about the hard skills that may be necessary in the industry. The student’s initial proposal had three modules under specialization learning which were cloud computing, user experience, and web development. However an opportunity on learning web 3 or blockchain development were given. Because of this, the student decided to replace the cloud computing module with blockchain instead.

The blockchain courses were offered by Metacrafters, which is a platform that allow their students to learn necessary skills and knowledge about blockchain development while earning rewards. The courses that the participants were required to take teaches about the languages used in blockchain development such as JavaScript and Solidity, as well as the different platforms like Etherium and Avalanche. Each of their courses also have project assessments that give learners an opportunity to put what they learned into practice. The next module is about user experience, this have courses that covers UX design and research that encourages its learners to take a user first approach in their designs. Finally, the last module is about web development. The courses in this module teaches about the technologies involved in developing web applications, this does not only cover about building websites but their deployment and development approaches as well.

Synthesis

In the duration of the practicum engagement, there are a number of things that the student learned from the courses that he took, and from the experiences that he had along the way. The first notable one is the experience of looking for possible host companies to train under. At first the student started to ask for letters of endorsement a few days before the start of the term to give himself more time. But even then, there were also a lot of idle time where the student was just waiting for responses from the companies that he approached. The student could also have made better decisions with choosing possible companies that could have given opportunities to gain proper training experience. This experience taught the student to be more mindful of the opportunities that are around him and become more proactive in these situations.

Even without the first hand training experience, the student gained knowledge in the learning path track that will still prove to be useful in the working environment. First are the mindsets that were taught in the required learning modules such as the concept of play. This concept is about encouraging oneself to be more playful with ideas without putting too much pressure on the possible consequences that it may lead to. This is not to say to start being irresponsible, but to allow room for failure and not put as much pressure as needed to the people we are working with. Play also enables exploring creative and innovative ideas, because big ideas are easier to downscale than trying to upscale something small.

The student also learned about the methodologies of project management, the discussions about the processes and documentation involved are huge eye openers. This is especially true with the course about waterfall, there is a large amount of mentioned documents that needs to be prepared in the planning phase and the processes that they have to go through from initiating the project to defining its scope. The student realized that the waterfall model is highly structured and strict with its processes. On the other hand, agile allows more flexibility and stakeholder engagement in order to provide features in a fast paced environment. And while agile is becoming more popular, this does not necessarily mean that there is no place for the traditional waterfall model.

Proper communication skills are very important in the workplace in order to build good relationships with colleagues and stakeholders. The student learned that as important as choice of words and tone of voice are, nonverbal communication is still very important. This is because it provides a lot of visual cues towards a person’s interest in the conversation and their current state of mind. This does not only mean gestures but also posture, eye contact, and even physical distance. Communication skills also apply to virtual messages such as emails and texts. Learning about when and how certain modes of communication are used is a good knowledge to acquire as it will serve as a good reference. In the time management courses that the student took, he noticed that it really aimed to solve the problems of people who has a large amount of tasks piled up. But it still provided useful information such as having gathering points of tasks in different mediums like a physical box or a virtual calendar, and that we should not use our minds as a storage for pending tasks as it makes us lose focus.

The courses in Metacrafters were very informative as all of it were new to the student. It was interesting to learn about how blockchain aims to revolutionize the web and the internet through decentralization where it is not run by major tech companies and everyone has a hold of their own assets. There is also the security involved in the blockchain which is centered in a hashing algorithm that converts data into a unique hash that even a single space or character change will modify the hash value. This allows the network to find out if the data has been tampered with. The way blockchain development is done is also quite interesting as it does not promote complex solutions even if it’s the most efficient because of gas usage. Gas is a unit used for computing transaction fees in the blockchain. For example, recursive functions are not advised and loops are kept to as little iterations as possible as they consume a lot of gas.

Accessibility is the greatest takeaway from the user experience module. It does not only mean having solutions deployed across different platforms, it mainly advocates inclusion of users with impairments. The user experience module focuses on designing projects to cater to their needs not just to reach a wider audience but to be able to help with alleviating their struggles in interacting with digital solutions. There are even proper standards that dictates how we should create accessible solutions such as the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG). The student realized that UI/UX is not just about having appealing visuals, a project’s designs should always have a user-first approach which is why UX research is also very important to have.

The web development module provided a variety of topics that covers the things necessary for building web applications. This does not only cover languages used for developing such as HTML, JavaScript and Django, but also concepts that we should be familiar with like REST APIs, SQL, deployment, and collaboration tools such as git. The student was already familiar with some of them but they still proved to be good refresher courses. On the other hand there are also topics that are new to the student such as how JavaScript is being used as a base for other platforms such as react.js and node.js, and while the way they are used differ, there is still a lot of JavaScript concepts at its core. The next one is Django that has somewhat of a unique approach in creating web applications and projects. It makes use of interacting with a lot of files when adding something new which was a bit confusing at first but it does allow for a more organized structure. As much as the student gained some insights on implementations with the number of libraries out there, he realized that he should have followed along the process of the video lessons instead of just listening and taking some notes. It may have took more time but it would have helped with having a deep understanding on how these concepts work first hand.

The student had a good learning experience throughout this learning path track despite not having trained under a host company. There were a lot of useful knowledge that the student gained especially in the area of soft skills which is as important as hard skills as it equips learners of the things that they should be aware of once they enter the working environment. And having this self-paced learning track also made the student realize that he still needs to work on his time management skills. Even though the student managed to finish on time, he was still behind his expected schedule and had to put extra effort in the last few days in order to keep up. Not only did the courses provide new information, but the whole experience in its entirety is a good lesson for the student to carry on.
